# FINDING: v1-full honest fail — frozen-base `hs_last` probe does not transfer to fresh Chef prompts

**Date:** 2026-04-09
**Branch:** DD v2 §11.6 frozen-base disposition sidecar
**Status:** falsified on the factual_accuracy capability anchor; other 4 axes pending sweep for completeness
**Scripts:** `dd-v2/eval/v1_full_v2.py`, `dd-v2/eval/v1_full_v2_batch_run.py`

## TL;DR

The v1a CV ceiling (AUC 0.683, balanced asym −0.341 on step16 n=100) was an **in-distribution CV artifact**. On 193 fresh Chef prompts (same domain, same model, same rubric), the probe collapses to chance:

| Metric | Bar | v1a CV (n=100, step16) | v1-full fresh (n=193) |
|---|---|---|---|
| AUC (factual correctness) | ≥ 0.63 | 0.683 / 0.663 (4-bit) | **0.516** |
| Balanced asym @ p_c ≥ 0.70 | ≤ −0.15 | −0.341 | **−0.043** |
| Gate | pass | pass | **fail** |

This falsifies the §11.6 premise at the capability anchor. Per `APPROACH_frozen_base_disposition_sidecar.md` decision matrix, this is the "signal exists in CV but not on fresh → frozen-base sidecar foundation is too weak" row. T1-lite is moot; the disposition probe sweep is only being run for completeness of the negative.

## Method (what was actually measured)

1. **Prompts (P1).** 193 fresh French-Chef error-correction prompts generated by DeepSeek V3.2-exp, matching the step16 type1 shape.
2. **Gold (P2).** Per-prompt canonical checklists (3–5 claims each) generated by the same judge; mirrors `eval_judge_rubric_v3.py` gold-checklist methodology.
3. **Responses (P4).** Gemma 4 E2B-it, Unsloth `load_in_4bit=True` (matches step16 dtype), `max_new_tokens=512`, greedy, left-padded batch=8. 193/193 kept.
4. **Features (P4).** Last-layer hidden state at the final generated (non-pad) token, per row. Extracted via a second forward pass over the full sequence under the same dtype.
5. **Labels (P5).** Per-claim YES/NO scoring against gold → coverage ∈ [0,1] → `label = coverage >= 0.5`. Fresh positive rate = 55/193 = 0.285.
6. **Gate training (P6–P7).** `StandardScaler → LogReg(C ∈ {1.0, 0.1})` on step16 hs_last (n=100, pos=58). Evaluation on the 193 fresh features.

Nothing about training or inference differs from the v1a 4-bit run that passed CV.

## Interpretation

**Root cause is visible in the samples, not just the metrics.** Gemma 4 E2B-it has a single output distribution on Chef error-correction prompts: verbose, structured, confident. Whether the model happens to hit 3/5 canonical facts (label=1) or 1/5 (label=0), the final-token hidden state encodes the same stylistic closure of a "verbose confident answer about French cooking", not the truth-value of the claims inside.

The v1a in-distribution CV AUC of 0.68 was therefore almost certainly picking up residual topic/length/category correlations within the 100 step16 items — artifacts that do not transfer once the prompts are drawn independently.

This is the **"hs_last is too weak a surface"** failure mode. The §11.6 theory ("the base model's final hidden state encodes a correctness-correlated direction") fails on factual correctness at this model scale + domain. There is no direction at that position to gate on.

## What this does NOT show

- It does not show that no correctness signal exists anywhere in the frozen base. Multi-layer pooling, earlier-token pooling, contrastive heads, or intermediate-layer MLP activations could still carry signal. The multi-layer probe variant is being run as a last cheap sanity-check before closing the branch.
- It does not show the other 3 disposition axes (hedging, pedagogical, self-verification, completeness) have no hs_last signal — but since the *capability anchor* (factual) is the axis most likely to carry a clean signal and it failed, strong priors say the others will also fail. The disposition_probe_sweep is being run for completeness to document this.
- It does not bear on SFT/DPO or on larger-model approaches. §11.6 was a *frozen-base*, *training-free*, *sidecar-only* claim. Its falsification does not reopen B.1/B.2 head-tempering (already falsified in §7) or imitation-into-weights (already closed).
